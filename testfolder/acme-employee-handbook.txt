SECTION 1: REFUND AND RETURNS POLICY
================================================================================

1.1 Standard Refund Policy
Customers may request a full refund within 30 days of purchase for any product
that is unused and in its original packaging. Refunds are processed within 5-7
business days to the original payment method. After 30 days, only store credit
is available.

1.2 Defective Products
If a product is found to be defective within 90 days of purchase, customers are
entitled to a free replacement or full refund regardless of the 30-day window.
Customers must provide proof of purchase and photos of the defect.

1.3 Digital Products
Digital downloads and software licenses are non-refundable once the download
link has been accessed. Exceptions are made only if the product is found to be
materially different from its description.

1.4 Shipping Costs
Original shipping costs are non-refundable unless the return is due to our error
or a defective product. Return shipping is the customer's responsibility unless
the item is defective.

SECTION 2: EMPLOYEE LEAVE POLICY
================================================================================

2.1 Annual Leave
Full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per year.
Part-time employees receive leave on a pro-rata basis. Leave must be approved
by the direct manager at least 2 weeks in advance, except in emergencies.

2.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical
certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. Unused sick
leave does not carry over to the next year.

2.3 Maternity and Paternity Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid maternity leave. Secondary
caregivers are entitled to 4 weeks of paid paternity leave. Adoption leave
follows the same policy as maternity/paternity leave.

2.4 Emergency Leave
Up to 3 days of paid emergency leave is available per year for immediate family
emergencies such as the death of a spouse, parent, child, or sibling.

SECTION 3: REMOTE WORK POLICY
================================================================================

3.1 Eligibility
Employees who have completed their 6-month probation period are eligible to
apply for remote work arrangements. Eligibility is subject to role requirements
and manager approval.

3.2 Remote Work Days
Approved remote workers may work from home up to 3 days per week. At least
2 days per week must be spent in the office to maintain team collaboration.
Fully remote arrangements require VP-level approval.

3.3 Equipment and Internet
The company provides a laptop and necessary peripherals for remote work.
Employees are responsible for maintaining a stable internet connection of at
least 25 Mbps. A monthly internet allowance of $50 is provided.

3.4 Security Requirements
Remote employees must connect via the company VPN at all times when accessing
internal systems. Using public Wi-Fi without VPN is strictly prohibited and
may result in disciplinary action.

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS
================================================================================

4.1 Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted twice a year: in June and December. The June
review is a mid-year check-in, and the December review determines year-end
bonuses and salary adjustments.

4.2 Rating Scale
Performance is rated on a 5-point scale:
  5 - Exceptional: Consistently exceeds all expectations
  4 - Exceeds Expectations: Frequently surpasses goals
  3 - Meets Expectations: Reliably achieves all goals
  2 - Needs Improvement: Partially meets expectations
  1 - Unsatisfactory: Fails to meet basic requirements

4.3 Bonus Structure
Employees rated 5 receive a 15% annual bonus. Employees rated 4 receive a 10%
bonus. Employees rated 3 receive a 5% bonus. Ratings of 2 or below do not
qualify for a bonus. Bonuses are paid in January of the following year.

4.4 Salary Increases
Merit-based salary increases are tied to the December review. Exceptional
performers (rating 5) receive up to 8% increase. Employees rated 4 receive
up to 5%. Employees rated 3 receive a standard 2-3% cost-of-living adjustment.

SECTION 5: IT AND DATA SECURITY POLICY
================================================================================

5.1 Password Policy
All passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include uppercase letters,
lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Passwords must be changed
every 90 days. Reuse of the last 10 passwords is prohibited.

5.2 Multi-Factor Authentication
MFA is mandatory for all employees accessing company systems. Approved MFA
methods include authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator)
and hardware security keys. SMS-based MFA is not permitted due to security risks.

5.3 Data Classification
Company data is classified into four levels:
  - Public: Can be freely shared externally
  - Internal: For employee use only, not for external sharing
  - Confidential: Restricted to authorized teams only
  - Top Secret: C-suite and legal access only

5.4 Incident Reporting
Any suspected security breach or data loss must be reported to the IT Security
team within 1 hour of discovery at security@acme.com. Failure to report a known
breach is a terminable offence.

SECTION 6: EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 Pre-Approval Requirement
All expenses over $500 require pre-approval from the department head before
being incurred. Expenses submitted without pre-approval may be denied.

6.2 Meal Allowances
Business meals with clients are reimbursable up to $75 per person. Team meals
(no clients) are reimbursable up to $30 per person. Alcohol is reimbursable
only for client entertainment, up to 20% of the total meal cost.

6.3 Travel Expenses
Economy class airfare is the standard for flights under 6 hours. Business class
is permitted for flights over 6 hours with VP approval. Hotel stays are
reimbursable up to $200 per night in standard cities and $300 in major metros
(New York, San Francisco, London, Tokyo).

6.4 Submission Deadline
Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred.
Reports submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed. All receipts must be
attached to the submission in the Expense Portal.

SECTION 7: CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

7.1 Workplace Respect
All employees are expected to treat colleagues, customers, and vendors with
respect and professionalism. Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any
kind will not be tolerated and may result in immediate termination.

7.2 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any personal relationships or financial interests that
may conflict with their professional responsibilities. This includes owning
shares in a competitor (more than 1%) or having a family member in a directly
competitive role.

7.3 Confidentiality
Employees must not disclose proprietary information, trade secrets, or
confidential client data to any external party, including after leaving the
company. All employees sign a non-disclosure agreement on their first day.

7.4 Social Media
Employees may not post content that identifies clients, shares confidential
company information, or disparages the company or its partners on social media.
Employees should clearly state that personal views are their own and not those
of ACME Corporation.
